# Blood Work Panel vs. Function Health — Price & Value Comparison

*Prepared 2026-06-13. Figures for Function Health are current as of June 2026; verify against functionhealth.com before quoting to patients, as pricing has changed twice in the past year.*

---

## TL;DR

| | **In‑house Panel** | **Function Health** |
|---|---|---|
| **Price** | **$216.30, one‑time** | **$365 / year** (subscription) |
| **Biomarkers** | ~70 markers, 1 draw | 100+ first draw, **160+ across the year** (2 draws) |
| **Draws per year** | 1 | 2 (baseline + ~3–6 mo. follow‑up of 60+ markers) |
| **Per‑marker cost** | ≈ **$3.09 / marker** | ≈ $3.65 / marker (first draw); ≈ $2.28 / marker over the full year |
| **Interpretation** | Clinician (functional‑medicine context) | Clinician‑reviewed notes + dashboard + biological age + AI coach |
| **Blood draw** | Per your lab arrangement (fee may be separate) | Quest Diagnostics included (2,000+ sites); home draw via Getlabs now rolling out |

**Bottom line:** For a *single, one‑time* comprehensive snapshot, the in‑house panel is cheaper — **$216.30 vs $365**. But because Function's $365 buys **two** draws a year, the math flips the moment a patient tests twice annually: **two in‑house panels = $432.60 vs Function's $365**. The real differences are subscription-vs-one-time, twice‑yearly tracking, and Function's software/longevity layer — not the per‑test price, which is nearly identical.

## 2. What Function Health includes ($365 / year)

- **100+ biomarkers** on the first draw, plus a **60+ marker** follow‑up draw 3–6 months later → **160+ lab tests/year**, two blood draws.
- Coverage spans **heart, hormones, thyroid, metabolic, nutrients, heavy metals, autoimmunity, liver, kidney, inflammation, and cancer‑signal markers**.
- **Clinician‑reviewed notes** after each draw, a **biological‑age** estimate, trend‑tracking **dashboard**, and an **AI health coach**.
- **Blood draw at Quest Diagnostics included** (2,000+ U.S. sites); **at‑home/mobile draw** rolling out after Function's April 2026 acquisition of Getlabs.
- **No per‑test or hidden fees** within the membership; à‑la‑carte add‑on tests are available at extra cost.

## 3. Marker‑by‑marker overlap

Function Health covers **essentially every marker on the in‑house panel** and then adds a large set the in‑house panel does not.

### Covered by both
CBC w/ differential & platelets · Lipid panel · Comprehensive metabolic panel (glucose, BUN, creatinine, eGFR, electrolytes, calcium, protein, albumin, globulin, bilirubin, ALP, AST, ALT) · GGT · LDH · Uric acid · TSH, Free T3, Free T4, Reverse T3, TPO & thyroglobulin antibodies · 25‑OH Vitamin D · Homocysteine · hs‑CRP · Iron, TIBC, Ferritin, Iron saturation · Hemoglobin A1c · Insulin · Folate · RBC Magnesium · ANA.

### On the in‑house panel, *not typically* in Function's standard set
- **T3 Uptake** and **Free Thyroxine Index** — older calculated thyroid indices. Function uses direct Free T3/Free T4 instead, so the clinical information is largely captured a different way.
- **Total T3 / Total T4** as discrete items (Function emphasizes the free fractions).
- **VLDL** as a separately reported value.

*These are presentation/derivation differences more than true coverage gaps — Function generally reports the modern direct equivalents.*

### In Function, *not* on the in‑house panel (examples)
- **Advanced cardiac:** Apolipoprotein B (ApoB), Lipoprotein(a) [Lp(a)], LDL particle size.
- **Nutrients/hormones:** Vitamin B12 / MMA, Cortisol, additional sex/adrenal hormones.
- **Heavy metals** (e.g., lead, mercury), **cancer‑signal markers** (e.g., PSA), and additional autoimmune/inflammation markers.

## 4. Cost analysis

### Per‑marker
- **In‑house:** $216.30 ÷ ~70 markers ≈ **$3.09 / marker**.
- **Function, first draw:** $365 ÷ 100 markers ≈ **$3.65 / marker**.
- **Function, full year:** $365 ÷ 160 markers ≈ **$2.28 / marker** (two draws counted).

### Annualized scenarios

| Testing cadence | In‑house cost | Function cost | Cheaper option |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Once / year** | $216.30 | $365.00 | **In‑house** (−$148.70) |
| **Twice / year** | $432.60 | $365.00 | **Function** (−$67.60) |
| **Over 3 yrs, once/yr** | $648.90 | $1,095.00 | **In‑house** (−$446.10) |
| **Over 3 yrs, twice/yr** | $1,297.80 | $1,095.00 | **Function** (−$202.80) |

> Note: the in‑house figures assume the draw/phlebotomy fee is either included or negligible. If a separate draw fee applies per visit, add it to each in‑house column — that narrows the once‑a‑year gap and widens Function's advantage on twice‑yearly testing (Function bundles the Quest draw at no extra charge).

## 5. Beyond price — what each model actually buys

**In‑house panel advantages**
- **Lowest cost for a one‑time, deep snapshot.** ~70 high‑value functional markers for $216.30 is hard to beat on price.
- **No subscription / no recurring commitment** — patient pays only when tested.
- **Functional‑medicine interpretation in a clinical relationship** rather than software notes; markers (Reverse T3, TPO/TGB antibodies, RBC magnesium, insulin, ANA, homocysteine) are already curated for a functional workup.
- You control the panel and can add/remove markers per patient.

**Function Health advantages**
- **Twice‑yearly tracking** built in — better for trend lines and intervention follow‑up.
- **Broader panel**: ApoB, Lp(a), B12, cortisol, heavy metals, cancer signals add screening reach.
- **Software layer**: dashboard, biological age, AI coach, longitudinal trends — a consumer‑friendly experience.
- **Logistics handled**: Quest draw included nationwide; home draw now rolling out.

---

## 6. Recommendation framing

- **If the goal is the cheapest thorough one‑time panel** → the in‑house panel wins clearly at **$216.30 vs $365**.
- **If the patient wants structured twice‑a‑year monitoring + a tracking platform** → Function's $365/year is competitive and actually **cheaper than two in‑house panels**, while adding markers and software.
- **For a functional‑medicine practice**, the in‑house panel also keeps interpretation, marker selection, and the patient relationship in‑house — value that doesn't show up in the per‑test math.

A reasonable hybrid: position the in‑house panel as the **lower‑cost, clinician‑guided** option, and note that its per‑marker price (~$3) is on par with Function's — patients are mostly paying Function for the **subscription cadence and software**, not cheaper labs.
